AOP works across four layers of a business: operations, cash flow, capital, and real estate. Each layer builds on the one before it. The financial services are integrated into active engagements and introduced at the right point in the client relationship, not offered on demand.

01

Fractional COO

Embedded Leadership · Execution Ownership · Operational Infrastructure

AOP embeds as your operational partner, taking direct ownership of the execution layer. We run the operational function, align the team, build the infrastructure for sustained performance, and stay until the structure holds on its own.

Active leadership without the cost or timeline of a full-time hire. For businesses that need a senior operator now and cannot wait to find and onboard one.

The Fractional COO engagement is also the entry point for the financial architecture work. Operational discipline is the foundation everything else is built on.

Where this applies: Leadership stretched across too many operational responsibilities, teams running without clear accountability, businesses preparing to scale and needing the infrastructure to support it.

What to expect

Direct, embedded involvement in your business. Clear ownership of operational outcomes. Honest communication about progress. A business that runs better after the engagement than before it.

02

Operational Assessment

Diagnosis · Constraint Identification · Prioritized Action Plan

A focused review of how the business actually operates. We look at how work moves, how decisions get made, and where the friction is costing time or revenue. The output is a clear picture of the current state and a specific, prioritized plan for what needs to change.

Where this applies: Something is clearly off but the source is not obvious, significant changes are being considered and an outside view is needed, the business is preparing for a growth phase and leadership wants to know what the operation can actually support.

What to expect

An honest picture of your operational reality. Specific findings, not general observations. A plan you can act on immediately with or without continued AOP involvement.

03

Targeted Improvement Initiatives

Defined Scope · Direct Execution · Measurable Outcome

Scoped engagements built around one operational problem. Sales process discipline, team accountability, workflow implementation, role alignment. AOP defines the scope, owns the execution, and delivers the outcome within a defined timeline.

Where this applies: A specific issue has resisted internal resolution, a targeted intervention is needed without the commitment of a full embedded engagement.

What to expect

A clearly scoped engagement with agreed outcomes and a defined timeline. AOP owns the work and delivers against what was committed to.

04

Operational Advisory

Standing Partner · Senior Perspective · Ongoing Support

Regular structured access to a senior AOP partner. We review what is happening in the business, pressure-test decisions, identify constraints before they become problems, and keep execution on track between larger engagements.

Where this applies: A senior operational perspective is needed on a standing basis, significant decisions require outside input, a previous engagement has concluded and continuity is valuable.

What to expect

A defined advisory relationship with clear structure. Regular sessions with a senior AOP partner who gives direct, honest input.

05

Revenue Recovery & Cash Acceleration

Cash Flow Management · Receivables Discipline · Collections Strategy

Revenue that has been earned but not collected is a structural problem, not a bookkeeping one. We assess where the breakdown is happening, implement a managed receivables process, and build the discipline to keep cash moving consistently from invoice to account.

Integrated into the operational engagement, not offered as a standalone service. Cash flow discipline is part of how a business operates. AOP owns the outcome and manages the process directly.

Where this applies: Receivables are growing, collection timelines are inconsistent, or cash position does not reflect actual revenue performance.

What to expect

A structured receivables process that is owned, managed, and monitored as part of the broader operational engagement. Measurable improvement in collection timelines and cash position.

06

Capital Strategy & Access

Strategic Capital Planning · Use of Funds Analysis · Vetted Partner Access

Before capital enters a business, three questions need clear answers: does it make strategic sense right now, is there a specific and defensible use for it, and can the business support it responsibly over time. AOP works through those questions first. When the answers are yes, we introduce vetted capital partners whose terms and structure align with the situation.

We stay involved in how capital is deployed. A capital event is a strategic move that carries the same accountability as any other operational decision. We treat it that way.

Where this applies: The business has a defined growth initiative that requires outside capital, the operational foundation is in place, and the use of funds is specific and tied to revenue. AOP does not introduce capital partners to businesses that are not ready for it.

What to expect

An honest assessment of whether capital makes sense for your situation. A defined use-of-funds strategy. Access to vetted partners only when the conditions are right. Continued involvement in deployment.

07

Real Estate Strategy for Business Growth

Ownership Analysis · Expansion Planning · Borrower Preparation · Lender Coordination

The decision to own commercial property instead of leasing it is a business strategy decision, not a real estate one. It depends on revenue stability, debt capacity, operational trajectory, and timing. AOP evaluates all of it before anything else happens.

When ownership does make sense, we define the right purchase range based on business performance, align property type with operational needs, prepare the borrower profile for lenders, and coordinate with vetted financing partners through execution. We stay involved after close to confirm the property is performing the way the strategy intended.

A significant part of the value here is identifying when a client is not ready. Preventing a poorly timed real estate decision protects the business and the capital that would have gone into it.

Where this applies: A business with stable revenue is considering expansion, evaluating ownership versus a long-term lease, or building toward a real estate position that strengthens the operating business over time.

What to expect

An honest assessment of readiness. A strategy built around your business performance, not the property. Coordination through the full process. Involvement after close to confirm the asset is performing as intended.
